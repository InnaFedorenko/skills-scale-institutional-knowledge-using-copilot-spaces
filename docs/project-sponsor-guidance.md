# Project Sponsor Guidance

**Audience:** Project Sponsors and executive stakeholders

**Purpose:** Describe the Project Sponsor role, how to support escalations, decision authority, recommended engagement cadence, and example actions for common scenarios.

Related docs:
- [Roles & Personas](./octoacme-roles-and-personas.md) — Project Sponsor persona definition and interaction model
- [Change Control Process](./change-control-process.md) — when Sponsor approval is required for change requests

---

## Role Summary

Project Sponsors are executive-level champions who ensure the project has the resources, organizational visibility, and strategic alignment needed to succeed. Sponsors provide authority to resolve decisions that exceed the Project Manager's scope and advocate for the project at the organizational level.

---

## Key Responsibilities

- Attend milestone reviews and scheduled sponsor check-ins
- Approve major scope, budget, and strategic priority changes
- Resolve escalated conflicts involving cross-organizational resources or competing priorities
- Advocate for the project at the executive and organizational level
- Ensure the project remains aligned with organizational strategy

---

## Decision Authority

Sponsors have final approval authority for:

| Decision Type | Authority Level |
|---|---|
| Major scope changes (>20% effort increase or new deliverables) | Sponsor approval required |
| Budget increases beyond the approved baseline | Sponsor approval required |
| Strategic trade-offs between competing organizational priorities | Sponsor decision |
| Cross-team resource allocation conflicts the PM cannot resolve | Sponsor intervention |
| Terminating or pausing a project | Sponsor decision |

Sponsors may **delegate** specific decisions in writing; delegated authority should be documented in the project decisions log.

---

## Escalation Guidance

The Project Manager escalates to the Project Sponsor when:
- A decision exceeds the PM's approved authority (schedule, budget, or cross-team resource allocation)
- A conflict between teams or stakeholders cannot be resolved at the PM level
- A major scope change is proposed that significantly affects timeline or cost
- An external factor (regulatory, organizational, or market) threatens the project's strategic alignment

**How to escalate:**
1. PM prepares a brief escalation summary: situation, options, recommended decision, and urgency
2. PM sends the summary to the Sponsor via the agreed communication channel (email, async tool, or a brief meeting)
3. Sponsor responds within the agreed SLA (see cadence below)
4. Decision is documented in the project decisions log by the PM

---

## Recommended Cadence

| Meeting Type | Frequency | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Sponsor check-in | Monthly (or per major milestone) | Review project health, risks, and upcoming decisions |
| Milestone review | Per milestone | Sponsor sign-off on milestone completion and next phase |
| Ad-hoc escalation | As needed | Resolve urgent decisions or unblock critical path items |

Sponsors should be reachable within **2 business days** for non-urgent escalations and **within 4 hours** for critical path blockers.

---

## Sponsor Quick Checklist

- [ ] Confirm the project is aligned with current organizational strategy
- [ ] Attend or delegate a representative to milestone reviews
- [ ] Review and sign off on major change requests within the agreed SLA
- [ ] Ensure the Project Manager has the resources needed for the current phase
- [ ] Provide timely feedback on escalations to avoid blocking the team

---

## Example Sponsor Actions for Common Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Resource Shortfall
Two teams are competing for the same senior engineer at a critical point in the project. The Project Manager has escalated because the conflict is cross-organizational.

**Sponsor action:**
- Meets briefly with both team leads and their managers
- Decides on a time-share arrangement or recruits additional funding to hire contract support
- Documents the decision and communicates it to both teams within 1 business day

---

### Scenario 2: Major Scope Change Request
A new regulatory requirement introduced mid-project doubles the implementation effort and pushes the delivery date by 6 weeks.

**Sponsor action:**
- Reviews the change request and impact assessment submitted by the Change Manager
- Approves the timeline extension and additional budget allocation
- Communicates the approved change to relevant organizational stakeholders
- Documents approval in the project decisions log

---

### Scenario 3: Strategic Realignment
The organization's leadership decides to pivot the product strategy, affecting two of the project's planned deliverables.

**Sponsor action:**
- Participates in a special milestone review with the Project Manager and Product Manager
- Decides which deliverables to retain, defer, or cancel based on the updated strategy
- Ensures the team is promptly informed of the revised scope and priorities

---

### Scenario 4: Project at Risk
The project is behind schedule by 3 weeks and the team has identified two critical risks with no current mitigation.

**Sponsor action:**
- Reviews the risk register and schedule impact summary from the Project Manager
- Approves additional resources or a revised scope to bring the project back on track
- Agrees to an increased check-in cadence (bi-weekly) until the project recovers
